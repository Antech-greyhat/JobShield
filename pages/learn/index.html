Learn & Protect

Educate yourself about common job scams in Kenya to stay safe.

The "Pay for Training" Scam

Legitimate employers will never ask you to pay for training or equipment before starting a job. If they ask for M-Pesa, run.

Fake Government Jobs

Scammers often pose as government officials offering positions in exchange for a "processing fee". Always verify via official .go.ke domains.

Overseas Employment Scams

Be wary of agencies promising lucrative jobs abroad while demanding upfront fees for visas and medical checks without verifiable contracts.

Interview or Application Fees

A real employer should not charge you to apply, attend an interview, reserve a slot, or get shortlisted for a role.

Too-Good-To-Be-True Salaries

Be cautious when entry-level jobs promise unusually high pay, instant hiring, free housing, or travel benefits with very little detail.

WhatsApp Recruitment Traps

Scammers may move you to WhatsApp quickly, pressure you to pay by M-Pesa, or avoid official email addresses and company contacts.

Fake Agency Registration

Before paying or sharing documents, confirm recruitment agencies through official business records, physical offices, and traceable contacts.

Identity Document Misuse

Do not send your ID, KRA PIN, passport, certificates, or bank details before verifying the employer and the job offer.

Remote Task Job Scams

Avoid jobs that ask you to deposit money, buy credits, or pay to unlock higher commissions for online tasks or product reviews.

How to Verify a Job

Search the company website, call official numbers, check LinkedIn activity, confirm office location, and compare the role across trusted job boards.
